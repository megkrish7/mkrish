Pulse- Enhancing Emergency Response

Problem Statement

Emergency situations are fraught with life-threatening delays and inefficiencies that significantly impact outcomes. Studies show that approximately 240 million calls are made to emergency services each year in the United States alone, with an estimated 10,000 annual deaths attributed to slow emergency response times. First responders face critical challenges in locating individuals quickly due to inaccurate or unavailable location data. Furthermore, the absence of immediate access to a person's medical history, such as known allergies, current medications, and underlying health conditions, can delay the administration of appropriate care and complicate medical decisions on the scene. Additionally, in many emergency scenarios, the victim's communication device may be damaged, rendering standard methods of calling for help ineffective.

Solution Developed

The Pulse app was designed to fundamentally transform emergency response communications by enabling rapid, reliable, and informed intervention from first responders. Developed using Swift for robust backend operations, SwiftUI for an intuitive user interface, and Firebase for real-time data synchronization, Pulse addresses the key challenges identified in emergency scenarios:

  • Immediate Location Sharing: Upon activation via a simple gesture, Pulse instantly transmits the user's precise GPS coordinates to emergency services, ensuring first responders can quickly locate the individual without delays.
  • Health Information Accessibility: Along with location data, the app automatically sends critical health information, including medical conditions, medications, allergies, and emergency contacts. This allows medical personnel to make informed treatment decisions upon arrival, tailoring their response to the individual's specific health needs.
  • Enhanced Communication in Compromised Conditions: Designed to function even when the device's screen is unresponsive, Pulse ensures that users can alert emergency services through predefined gestures, bypassing the need for verbal communication or traditional dialing methods, which may not be possible in all situations.

This solution not only enhances the speed of emergency responses but also improves the quality of care provided during critical moments, potentially saving lives and reducing the severity of medical outcomes. By integrating sophisticated technology with a user-centric design, Pulse redefines emergency communication, offering an innovative and practical tool that addresses the vital needs of both emergency victims and responders.

Technical Challenges and Solutions

Real-Time Data Synchronization

Challenge: A critical requirement for Pulse is the ability to synchronize data such as user location and medical information in real time between the user’s device and first responders.

Solution: We utilized Firebase Realtime Database, which provides real-time data synchronization. Every time data is added or modified in the database, it automatically updates across all devices, ensuring that first responders receive immediate updates without any delay.

User Interface Responsiveness and Accessibility

Challenge: The app needed to be highly responsive and accessible, allowing users to send distress signals through simple gestures, even under stressful conditions.

Solution: SwiftUI was employed to build a clean and uncluttered user interface. The declarative syntax of SwiftUI made it straightforward to implement a responsive design. Accessibility features such as VoiceOver and larger touch targets were incorporated to ensure the app could be used easily by everyone, including those with disabilities.

Secure Storage and Handling of Sensitive Information

Challenge: Handling and securely storing sensitive personal health information (PHI) was paramount, given the privacy concerns and regulatory requirements.

Solution: Firebase’s security rules were meticulously configured to ensure that data access was strictly regulated. Data encryption at rest and in transit was implemented to protect PHI. Additionally, user authentication was managed through Firebase Authentication, providing robust security measures such as two-factor authentication to safeguard user accounts.

Reliability of Emergency Notifications

Challenge: It was essential for the emergency notifications triggered by the app to be highly reliable and delivered without fail across various network conditions.

Solution: Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) was integrated to handle push notifications reliably. We also implemented redundant notification systems and network condition checks to ensure notifications were sent even in less optimal conditions.

Handling Offline Scenarios

Challenge: The app needed to function effectively even when the user’s device was offline or in poor network conditions.

Solution: The offline capabilities of Firebase were leveraged, which allow the applications to keep functioning while offline. The app caches data and synchronizes it with Firebase once the device reconnects to the internet, ensuring no data is lost and all emergency signals are sent as soon as online connectivity is restored.
